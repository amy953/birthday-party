Common Questions

What Parents Ask Us Most

Honest answers before you book.

"What if my child is scared of animals?"

Completely normal — and we handle it gently every time. No child is ever required to interact before they're ready. Our farm host reads each child and moves at their pace. Most nervous kids are the ones asking to hold the chick by the end.

"What happens if it rains?"

We have covered and indoor space available for every party. A little Tennessee weather won't cancel the fun — we move the party inside and the animals are still there. We've never had to cancel a birthday.

"Can we bring our own food and decorations?"

Absolutely. Bring your cake, your balloons, your favorite snacks — the space is yours for the full two hours. We provide tables and chairs. You make it feel like a birthday.

"Is this good for mixed ages — toddlers through tweens?"

Yes. We host all ages, and the farm genuinely works for every kid differently. Toddlers love the sensory experience. Older kids get more confident with the animals. The craft scales too — everyone finds their version of it.

"How far in advance do I need to book?"

We recommend at least 3 weeks out, especially for weekend dates in spring and fall. Summer weekends book fast. If you have a specific date in mind, book early — your date is secured when payment is made in full.

"Can adults enjoy it too?"

Honestly, yes. Parents almost always end up right in the middle of the animal experience themselves. The farm has a way of doing that. You'll want to be there — not just watching from the side.
